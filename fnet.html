fnet

A popular choice for those wanting to do networking in C and C++ is the enet library, which provides "a relatively thin, simple and robust network communication layer on top of UDP". I created fnet to simplify this layer even further into just two C++ classes: fnet::Server and fnet::Client. These classes provide just a handful of very useful methods, such as Send (send a message to a client), Broadcast (send a message to all clients) and Ping (ping a client). I tested this design in a small tank battle game, with good results.

I started working on fnet out of necessity, but it eventually became an interesting exercise in class interface design. I had recently read Scott Meyers' Effective C++, which advocates "minimal and complete" class interfaces. There's no doubt that fnet's interface is minimal, but I'll leave judgement on its completeness until I use it in a real program.
